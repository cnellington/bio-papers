# bio-papers

A list of 30 papers to understand 90% of modern biology.

This list focuses on papers that define the edges of biology, where methodological or intellectual paradigms changed hands.

1. Outeiro, Tiago Fleming, and Susan Lindquist. 2003. “Yeast Cells Provide Insight into Alpha-Synuclein Biology and Pathobiology.” *Science* (New York, N.Y.) 302 (5651): 1772–75. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1090439.
2. Altshuler, David, Mark J. Daly, and Eric S. Lander. 2008. “Genetic Mapping in Human Disease.” *Science (New York, N.Y.)* 322 (5903): 881–88. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1156409.
3. Barrangou, Rodolphe, Christophe Fremaux, Hélène Deveau, et al. 2007. “CRISPR Provides Acquired Resistance against Viruses in Prokaryotes.” *Science* (New York, N.Y.) 315 (5819): 1709–12. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1138140.
4. Cong, Le, F. Ann Ran, David Cox, et al. 2013. “Multiplex Genome Engineering Using CRISPR/Cas Systems.” *Science* (New York, N.Y.) 339 (6121): 819–23. https://doi.org/10.1126/science.1231143.
5. Durrant, Matthew G., Nicholas T. Perry, James J. Pai, et al. 2024. “Bridge RNAs Direct Programmable Recombination of Target and Donor DNA.” *Nature* 630 (8018): 984–93. https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-07552-4.
6. Bray, Mark-Anthony, Shantanu Singh, Han Han, et al. 2016. “Cell Painting, a High-Content Image-Based Assay for Morphological Profiling Using Multiplexed Fluorescent Dyes.” *Nature Protocols* 11 (9): 1757–74. https://doi.org/10.1038/nprot.2016.105.
